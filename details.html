Project Outline

Being sarcastic is to say something and to mean something else. Detecting sarcasm is key for social media analysis to differentiate between the two opposite polarities that an utterance may convey. Different techniques for detecting sarcasm are varying from rule-based models to Machine Learning and Deep Learning models. However, researchers tend to leverage Deep Learning in detecting sarcasm recently. On the other hand, the Arabic language has not witnessed much improvement in this research area.
Bridging the gap in sarcasm detection of the Arabic language is the target behind this work. In this work, efficient models in short text classification are tested for detecting sarcasm in the Arabic language for the first time. The dataset used to train and test these different architectures was manually collected by scrapping two different websites, sarcastic and non-sarcastic. The state-of-the-art results among these models were achieved by the CNN-LSTM model with an accuracy of 0.928 and F1 score of 0.931.

Motivation

Why to detect sarcasm?

  • To determine right polarity of a sentence in sentiment analysis tasks.
  • Diagnoses of depression.
  • For autistic people: who are often victims of bullying.

Goals

We aim at bridging the gap of sarcasm detection task in Arabic language. The proposed model has to determine sentence category (weather it is sarcastic or not), with competitive results in contrast to other models.

Background

Data Scraping

Dataset used in this paper is built by scrapping headlines news; covering different topics: political, social, economic… etc. Web scraping is a beneficial tool to extract data for websites without API’s.

For sarcastic headlines, we have scraped the Arabic sarcastic website named *“alhudood.net”, using Scrapy python library (an open source and powerful framework for data crawling). For non-sarcastic headlines, we have scraped the Arabic news website “masress.com”.

Our dataset is written in standard Arabic and is balanced, with 2999 sarcastic headlines and 2999 non-sarcastic ones. *Dataset will be available for download for research purposes.

Features (Word Representation)

Text classification is to label different texts according to common patterns or features. A substantial concept in sarcasm detection is to look for sentiment words that cases incongruity in an utterance. On Contrary, some sentences convey sarcasm with no existence of such words. Therefore, the role of word embedding (representation) arises. Word embedding as a feature is a numerical representation for words in fixed size vectors. Additionally, it is an avenue to discover sematic and syntactic information. Most common used pre-trained word embedding are: word2vec by Google, glove by Stanford, and fast Text by Facebook.

We preferred using word embedding rather than one-hot vectors. Researchers at Facebook AI Research leveraged from skipgram in word embedding. They throw lights to represent language at a character-level. They proposed representations for each word as bag of characters, with vector representation assigned to each character n-gram and word representation as a sum of its n-gram vectors. Another pertained model for Arabic word embedding, called “AraVec”. AraVec provides variant word representation models for different text domains, they utilized Continuous Bag of Words (CBOW) and Skipgram. Their proposed work provides 16 different word embedding models using two content platforms Twitter and Wikipedia. Tweets to represent bilingual Arabic and Wikipedia for standard Arabic.

CNN Architectures

CNN Architecture

The CNN Architecture. Recent Deep Learning researchers have revealed that CNN is remarkably powerful in classification tasks. First, a representation of headlines is performed using FastText. For each input headline, each token is represented by the corresponding embedding vector to construct the embedding matrix. Consequently, an input sentence can be treated as an image. Then three of 2D convolution layers are applied on that headline using six convolutional filters. Each filter generated a feature map. A max pooling layer is added to get a fixed-length vector from the preceding feature maps. Then, resultant outputs of the preceding stage are concatenated into a fixed-size filter map. Finally, this filter map is fed through a softmax function to determine whether the input headline is sarcastic or non-sarcastic.

RNN Architecture - LSTM

LSTM as a special type of RNN that is efficient in solving the vanishing and exploding gradients problems. Simple RNN architectures suffer from such gradient problems. For standard RNN models, the cell is one layer and almost tanh. However, in this model, an LSTM cell is represented in more complex structures with a higher number of gates to update memory. By keeping important information and forgetting others, LSTM is much eager to solve the problem of long-term dependencies. In this proposed LSTM model, the output of the embedding layer is fed to two LSTM layers, then passed to two fully connected layers (DNN) and finally, to the softmax layer. Also, a dropout layer was added after each layer with a probability of 0.5 to solve the over-fitting problem that occurs mainly because of the small dataset size. While LSTM learns only dependencies of the preceding word’s information, a crucial rule for utilizing BiLSTM was revealed. BiLSTM has the ability to learn the dependencies of both the preceding and following information of a word in a sentence. Adding a BiLSTM layer instead of the two LSTM layers in the previous architecture was experimented.

Hybrid Architecture

Building this approach represents an avenue to leverage the advantages of both CNN and LSTM architectures. LSTM architecture is efficient in feature extraction and semantic analysis. While CNN architecture is more efficient in the accuracy of classification. Hybrid models implemented in this work are represented in different architectures as follows: First, the structure of this proposed model is as follows: a CNN architecture on top of a multi-layer LSTM model. Second, the LSTM architecture on top of a CNN model. Finally, the third one has a BiLSTM architecture on top of a CNN model.
